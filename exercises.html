Phase 1 Pre-surgery prehabilitation 2–4 weeks before surgery
Easy
Quad sets
10 reps × 3 setsIn bed
Strengthens the quadriceps without stressing the hip joint.
How to do it
1

Lie flat on your back with legs straight.

2

Tighten the muscles on top of your thigh by pressing the back of your knee into the bed.

3

Hold for 5 seconds, then relax fully.

4

Repeat 10 times on each leg. Rest 1 minute between sets.

Easy
Straight leg raise
10 reps × 3 setsIn bed
Builds hip flexor and quad strength before surgery.
How to do it
1

Lie on your back. Bend one knee with foot flat, keep the other leg straight.

2

Tighten the quad of the straight leg, then raise it to the height of the bent knee.

3

Hold 2 seconds at the top, then lower slowly.

4

Complete 10 reps per side per set.

Moderate
Mini squats
10 reps × 3 setsNear wall
Strengthens quads, glutes, and hip stabilizers using a wall for balance.
How to do it
1

Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, lightly touching a wall for support.

2

Slowly bend your knees to about 30°, keeping your back straight.

3

Hold 2 seconds, then straighten back up slowly.

4

Stop if you feel hip pain. Do not squat past 90°.

Easy
Glute squeezes
10 reps × 3 setsLying down
Activates the gluteal muscles and improves pelvic stability.
How to do it
1

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor.

2

Squeeze your buttock muscles together as tightly as comfortable.

3

Hold for 5 seconds, then release completely.

4

Repeat 10 times per set. Breathe normally throughout.

Phase 2 Hospital stay Days 1–3 post-surgery
Easy
Ankle pumps
20 reps every hourIn bed
Prevents blood clots and keeps circulation moving during bed rest.
How to do it
1

Sit or lie with legs extended in front of you.

2

Pull your toes toward your shins (flex), then push them away (point).

3

One flex + one point = one rep. Move smoothly.

4

Do 20 reps every hour while awake, especially the first two weeks.

Easy
Heel slides
10 reps × 3 setsIn bed
Gently restores hip and knee flexion range of motion.
How to do it
1

Lie on your back with both legs straight.

2

Slowly slide the heel of your operated leg toward your buttock by bending the knee.

3

Go as far as comfortable — do not force. Hold 2 seconds.

4

Slowly straighten back to start. Repeat 10 times.

Easy
Sitting to standing
5–10 reps × 2 setsWith walker
Rebuilds the functional movement pattern needed for daily life.
How to do it
1

Sit at the front edge of a firm chair, feet flat, walker in front.

2

"Nose over toes" — lean forward and push through the armrests to stand.

3

Grip the walker once standing. Balance for 5 seconds before moving.

4

Reverse slowly to sit: back up until you feel the chair, then lower gradually.

Easy
Short walks with walker
2–3× dailyHospital corridor
Restores weight-bearing confidence and prevents post-surgical complications.
How to do it
1

Have a nurse or PT present for your first few attempts.

2

Advance the walker, then step your operated leg forward. Follow with the other leg.

3

Walk at a slow, steady pace. Look ahead, not down.

4

Start with 50 feet and increase distance as comfort allows.

Phase 3 Early recovery at home Weeks 1–6 post-surgery
Easy
Standing hip abduction
10 reps × 3 setsNear wall
Strengthens hip abductors — essential for walking stability.
How to do it
1

Stand upright holding a wall or chair. Keep your back straight.

2

Slowly lift your operated leg out to the side, toes forward.

3

Lift to hip height only — do not lean sideways.

4

Hold 2 seconds, lower slowly. 10 reps per side.

2

Slowly straighten your operated knee until your leg is horizontal.

3

Hold 2–3 seconds, feeling the quad engage.

4

Lower slowly — do not let the foot drop. 10 reps per set.

Moderate
Hip marching (seated)
10 reps × 3 setsFirm chair
Improves hip flexor strength and coordination for walking.
How to do it
1

Sit tall in a firm chair with both feet flat on the floor.

2

Lift the knee of your operated leg as high as comfortable — aim for 90°.

3

Hold 2 seconds, then lower under control. Do not lean back.

4

Alternate legs. 10 reps each side = 1 set.

Moderate
Standing hip extension
10 reps × 3 setsNear wall
Strengthens the gluteus maximus and hip extensors for walking power.
How to do it
1

Stand facing a wall, hands lightly resting on it for balance.

2

Slowly swing your operated leg straight back behind you.

3

Move from the hip — do not arch your lower back.

4

Hold 2 seconds at the back, return slowly. 10 reps per side.

Phase 4 Active rehabilitation Weeks 6–12 post-surgery
Moderate
Step-ups
10 reps × 3 setsBottom stair
Builds functional leg strength for climbing stairs confidently.
How to do it
1

Stand at the bottom of a step, holding the railing for support.

2

Lead with your non-operated leg. Step up, then bring the operated leg to join.

3

Step back down leading with the operated leg.

4

"Up with the good, down with the bad." 10 full cycles = 1 set.

Challenging
Stationary bike
10–20 minGym or home
Low-impact cardio that improves range of motion and endurance.
How to do it
1

Set the seat high so your hip only bends slightly at the top of the pedal stroke.

2

Use minimal resistance for the first two weeks. Focus on smooth pedaling.

3

Start with 10 minutes and build to 20 minutes over 2–3 weeks.

4

Stop if you feel groin or hip pain. Increase resistance gradually.

Moderate
Side-lying clamshells
15 reps × 3 setsFloor or bed
Targets hip external rotators and glute medius for deep stability.
How to do it
1

Lie on your non-operated side with hips stacked, knees bent to 45°.

2

Keeping feet together, rotate your top knee upward like a clamshell opening.

3

Raise as high as possible without rolling your pelvis backward.

4

Hold 2 seconds at the top, lower slowly. 15 reps per side.

Challenging
Partial lunges
8 reps × 3 setsNear wall
Rebuilds strength, balance, and hip mobility for functional movement.
How to do it
1

Stand near a wall for support. Step your non-operated leg forward.

2

Slowly lower your body by bending both knees — go as deep as comfortable.

3

Keep your front knee over your ankle and your torso upright.

4

Push back up to standing. 8 reps per side per set.

Important safety note

Hip precautions vary by surgical approach (posterior vs. anterior). Always confirm with your surgeon and physical therapist which movements to avoid. Stop any exercise that causes sharp pain, increased swelling, or a clicking sensation in the hip joint.
