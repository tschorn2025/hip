Pre-surgery checklist

Get ready for your surgery

Check off each item as you complete it. Items are grouped by how far in advance you should act. Your progress is tracked below.

Medical appointments

0/7
Attend pre-operative evaluation with your surgeon
Review x-rays, implant choice, and surgical plan. Bring a list of questions.
4 weeks out
Complete pre-op blood work and clearance tests
ECG, CBC, metabolic panel — your surgical team will specify what's needed.
4 weeks out
Get cardiac or specialist clearance if required
If you have a heart condition, diabetes, or other chronic illness.
4 weeks out
Attend pre-operative physical therapy session
Learn post-op exercises and mobility techniques before surgery.
2 weeks out
Review all medications with your surgeon
Blood thinners, NSAIDs, and supplements may need to be paused before surgery.
2 weeks out
Attend pre-admission nursing assessment
Anesthesia review, allergy confirmation, and consent forms.
1 week out
Complete any required dental work beforehand
Active dental infection can pose infection risk post-implant.
4 weeks out

Home preparation

0/8
Arrange a raised toilet seat or grab bars in the bathroom
You won't be able to bend your hip past 90° initially.
2 weeks out
Set up a recovery area on the ground floor if possible
Minimizes stair use during early recovery.
2 weeks out
Remove trip hazards — loose rugs, cables, clutter
Falls are the most common post-op complication at home.
1 week out
Purchase a long-handled reacher, sock aid, and shoe horn
Essential tools for dressing independently without bending.
2 weeks out
Secure a walker or crutches ahead of surgery
Check with your surgical team which aid they recommend.
1 week out
Prepare and freeze meals for the first two weeks
Standing to cook will be limited early in recovery.
1 week out
Install a stable handrail on your main staircase if not present
You'll need to hold the rail on every stair trip initially.
2 weeks out
Set up phone charger, remote controls, and essentials at seat height
Avoid repeated bending down to pick things up.
1 week out

Logistics and support

0/6
Arrange a responsible adult to drive you home after surgery
You cannot drive after general or spinal anesthesia.
2 weeks out
Arrange post-op care support for at least the first two weeks
A family member, friend, or professional carer to assist with meals, errands, and transport.
2 weeks out
Notify your employer and arrange medical leave
Most desk workers return in 4–6 weeks; physical roles take longer.
4 weeks out
Arrange care for children or pets during hospital stay
You'll be in hospital 1–3 days and unable to lift or bend for several weeks after.
1 week out
Confirm your surgery time and hospital check-in details
Know where to go, what time to arrive, and who to contact.
1 week out
Book post-op physiotherapy appointments in advance
PT slots fill quickly — schedule at least 3 sessions for weeks 1–3.
2 weeks out

Loose, comfortable clothing and non-slip footwear

Day before

Day before
Phone, charger, headphones, and comfort items
You'll likely be in hospital 1–3 nights.
Day before
Toiletries, lip balm, and reading material

Physical preparation

0/4
Begin prehabilitation exercises
Stronger muscles before surgery lead to faster recovery after. See the exercise library →
4 weeks out
Stop smoking at least 4 weeks before surgery
Smoking significantly increases risk of infection and slows bone healing.
4 weeks out
Follow any pre-op fasting instructions
Typically nothing to eat after midnight. Confirm exact instructions with your team.
Day before
Shower with antiseptic wash the night before and morning of surgery
Reduces skin bacteria and infection risk at the incision site.
Day before

Key questions to ask your surgeon

Which surgical approach will you use — posterior or anterior? What hip precautions will I need? When can I drive, return to work, and resume sports? What are the signs of infection or dislocation to watch for?
